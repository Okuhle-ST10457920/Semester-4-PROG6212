Types of Student Funding

Here's a breakdown of what each student funding option entails:

Bursary

Financial awards given to students
based on academic merit, financial need, or specific
criteria set by the awarding institution or organization.
Typically don't require repayment.
May have conditions attached, such as working for a specific company after
graduation.
Can cover tuition fees, accommodation,
living expenses, and other study-related costs.

Student Loan

Financial assistance provided to students to cover education
expenses, with the understanding that the loan will be repaid
with interest.
Eligibility criteria often include financial need,
academic performance, and creditworthiness.
Repayment terms vary, but typically begin after graduation.
Can be offered by government institutions, private banks, or other
financial entities.

Learnership

Work-based learning programs that combine theoretical and practical training.
Often involve a contract between the learner, employer, and training provider.
Can provide students with valuable work experience and skills.
May include financial support or stipends for learner.
